Welcome to Arizona Fly Fishing

Arizona offers surprising variety for anglers: cold mountain streams with wild trout, desert reservoirs full of bass and catfish, and high-country lakes that feel worlds away from the Sonoran lowlands. This site helps you learn where to go, what to tie on, and how to plan a day on the water. Whether you are new to the sport or have waded these runs for years, you will find practical ideas for your next trip.

Popular species include rainbow and brown trout in the upland creeks, largemouth and smallmouth bass in warm reservoirs, and channel catfish in many public lakes. Seasonal flows and water temperatures change quickly in Arizona, so timing and fly choice matter as much as location. Explore our Rivers and Lakes pages for access notes and seasonal tips, and visit AZ Fly Shop for regional reports and gear.
